# Beginner To Intermediate

## What Progress Looks Like

- You stop reaching for `String` and raw numbers by default.
- You see constructors as invariant boundaries.
- You can explain a module in terms of responsibility, not file location.
- You ask where logic belongs before writing it.
- You begin to use tests to protect design, not just output.

## Common Friction At This Stage

- wanting traits too early
- overusing clones to quiet ownership pressure
- confusing "more abstraction" with "better design"
- treating the compiler as an enemy instead of feedback

## Coaching Focus

- strengthen domain thinking
- make ownership legible
- reduce procedural drift
- build confidence in small architectural decisions
